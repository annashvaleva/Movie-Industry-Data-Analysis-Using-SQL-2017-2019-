MOVIE INDUSTRY DATA ANALYSIS — RESULTS SUMMARY
=============================================

Dataset: Movies released between 2017 and 2019
Source: Excel dataset imported into SQL Server
Total movies analyzed: ~7,997


--------------------------------------------------
QUESTION 1: DATA UNDERSTANDING
--------------------------------------------------

• The database consists of three core tables: movies, ratings, and genres.
• The movies table contains ~7,997 unique films.
• The genres table contains 14,662 records, reflecting a many-to-one relationship (movies can have multiple genres).
• The ratings table contains one rating record per movie.
• The dataset covers movies released between 2017 and 2019.

Insight:
The dataset is well-structured for relational analysis and represents a recent, focused time period suitable for trend analysis.


--------------------------------------------------
QUESTION 2: RELEASE TRENDS & RATINGS
--------------------------------------------------

• Number of movies released per year:
  - 2017: 3,052 movies
  - 2018: 2,944 movies
  - 2019: 2,001 movies

• Average movie rating per year:
  - 2017: 5.75
  - 2018: 5.76
  - 2019: 5.96

Insight:
While the number of movie releases decreased over time, the average rating increased, suggesting an improvement in perceived movie quality despite lower production volume.


--------------------------------------------------
QUESTION 3: MOST POPULAR MOVIES
--------------------------------------------------

• Top movies by total number of votes include:
  - Avengers: Infinity War
  - Avengers: Endgame
  - Logan
  - Black Panther
  - Joker

Insight:
High-budget franchise movies dominate audience engagement, indicating strong brand recognition and fan loyalty.


--------------------------------------------------
QUESTION 4: GENRE ANALYSIS — RATINGS
--------------------------------------------------

• Genres with the highest average ratings:
  - Drama (6.21)
  - Romance (5.99)
  - Family (5.95)
  - Crime (5.93)

• Genres with lower average ratings:
  - Horror (4.86)
  - Sci-Fi (5.07)

Insight:
Drama and Romance genres tend to receive higher critical appreciation, while genres like Horror and Sci-Fi show lower average ratings.


--------------------------------------------------
QUESTION 5: GENRE ANALYSIS — POPULARITY VS QUALITY
--------------------------------------------------

• Genres with the largest audience engagement (total votes):
  - Drama: 27M+
  - Action: 22M+
  - Adventure: 17M+

• High audience engagement does not always correlate with high ratings.

Insight:
Action and Adventure attract large audiences despite lower average ratings, while Drama successfully combines high ratings with strong audience engagement.


--------------------------------------------------
OVERALL BUSINESS CONCLUSIONS
--------------------------------------------------

• A decline in movie production does not imply a decline in quality.
• Drama emerges as the most successful genre in terms of both ratings and audience reach.
• Audience engagement and perceived quality do not always align.
• Genre-level analysis provides valuable insights for production, marketing, and investment decisions.
